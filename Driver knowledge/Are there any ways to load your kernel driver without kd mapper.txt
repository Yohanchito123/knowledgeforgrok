dse bypass but walking PsLoadedModuleList would still get you tapped afaik
another good (but expensive) option is purchasing your own legitimate certificate and using it to sign ur driver.

if you want a decent (and free) alternative i suggest spending some time finding a legitimate signed kernel driver that has r/w memory access, reverse engineer it, and build your own manual mapper around it. it is a better choice than kdmapper but if you don't do it right there will be some detection vectors left. depending on the type of mapper you build your driver code may or may not be backed by a legitimate kernel module (and if it isnt thats a huge detection vector).

if i recall correctly, theres basically 2 main types of driver mappers: you can find a driver with a big enough .rdata / .data (or even .text but thats not great) section and map your target driver in there, or do what kdmapper does (and what i mentioned above) which is to find a driver with r/w, allocate phys memory and call your driver's driver entry.

both of these require a legitimate kernel driver, and depending on the path you choose it will require you to find one with r/w privileges. remember both of these options come with pros and cons.

truth is you can't be 100% undetected these days. the main idea is reducing as many detection vectors as possible. hope i was of some help

Originally Posted by Seari7 View Post
Hello everyone, I would like to ask about how to load my driver into the system to bypass anti-cheats. I know that there is kdmapper, but is it worth writing my own driver similar to kdmapper, or should I just try to rewrite the existing kdmapper?
The only ways to load your driver is by mapping it manually or going through the legitimate launch path whether that be with a dse bypass or valid signature. Kdmapper is not the only option for mapping your driver, you could make your own or look at other public releases. You just need to get some memory, write your driver to it, make it executable (if it isn't already) and execute it. There are many ways to get memory for your driver but the most likely you're going to allocate it with ExAllocatePool, MmAllocateIndependentPages, MmAllocateContiguousMemory, etc.. There are also a few choices for executing it too like overwriting a syscall like NtAddAtom (kdmapper method), hijacking ioctls, a dataptr hook, etc. the last 2 "execution" methods are usually called communication methods but you could use them to execute your entry point if you wanted to. Some people don't even have entry points because they use a static stub rather than an entire driver, and for something like that the data ptr hook becomes a permanent solution. But most people using a data ptr hook using another execution method to execute their driver ep and then setup the dataptr hook in their ep for their communication
